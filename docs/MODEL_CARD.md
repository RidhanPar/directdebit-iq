# DirectDebit IQ Model Card

## Intended Use

DirectDebit IQ is a portfolio prototype for ranking scheduled direct-debit payments by failure risk. It is designed to support analyst review, retry prioritisation, and payment-success exploration.

It must not be used to automatically reject, delay, or alter real customer payments without production validation, governance, and human oversight.

## Data

- Training data: synthetic payment records generated by `data/generate_data.py`.
- Target: whether a payment succeeds or fails.
- Features: payment value, timing, mandate age, previous failures, customer history, merchant history, bank geography/type, and balance band.
- Known limitation: synthetic patterns may not transfer to real merchants or customers.

## Validation

- Evaluation uses an out-of-time holdout containing the most recent 20% of payments.
- Primary metrics: average precision, ROC-AUC, precision, recall, F1, and revenue-at-risk coverage.
- Production validation should additionally report results by merchant, country, payment-value band, and customer tenure.

## Risks and Controls

- False positives may create unnecessary customer contact or operational work.
- Historical data may encode merchant or geographic bias.
- Model performance and calibration may drift over time.
- Actions should remain reviewable, explainable, monitored, and reversible.

## Monitoring Recommendation

Monitor data quality, score distribution, calibration, average precision, recall at fixed review capacity, merchant-level performance, and operational outcomes from retry actions.
